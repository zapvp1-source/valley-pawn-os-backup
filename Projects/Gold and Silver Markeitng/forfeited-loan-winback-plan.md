# Forfeited-Loan Win-Back — Long-Term Messaging Plan

**Valley Pawn · "We're always here when you need us."**
Status: Strategy brief v1 · Approach locked: **emotional / relationship-only (no incentive)**

---

## The one-line idea

When a customer loses a pawned item to forfeiture, they often feel a quiet shame — like they failed. **We flip that.** Losing an item isn't a failure, it's exactly what pawn is *for*: it gave you cash when you needed it, no credit check, no debt collector, no hit to your credit. The collateral did its job. And the door is open the same way it always was — **no shame, no judgment, we're here whenever you need us again.**

This is a *relationship* play, not a discount play. We are not buying these customers back. We are removing the emotional reason they don't come back on their own.

---

## Why this works (the insight)

A forfeited loan is one of the few "negative" moments in pawn — and it's almost always silent. The customer doesn't call to say "I couldn't get my ring back." They just go quiet, sometimes for years, because they associate us with the loss and assume *we* think less of them.

Three truths reframe it:

1. **The loan worked exactly as designed.** No credit check, no collections, no credit-score damage. They got what they needed; the item covered it. That's the *point* of collateral lending.
2. **Forfeiture is a clean break, not a debt.** Unlike a bank or a payday lender, when a pawn loan ends, it ends. Nothing follows them home. That's a feature worth naming out loud.
3. **They are a *proven* customer.** They've trusted us with something they valued. The hardest part — getting someone to walk in the first time — is already done.

The shame is the only thing standing between "lost an item" and "came back next time they needed cash or wanted to shop." Our entire job here is to dissolve that shame before they need us again, so that when the moment comes, Valley Pawn is the warm, obvious choice.

This ladders directly into the brand: **"What's Right Is Right,"** customer-first, family-owned, *"the people who say yes when the bank says no."*

## Message platform — the words

### Core promise (the through-line on every channel)
> **"No judgment. No catch. We're here whenever you need us."**

### Supporting lines (rotate; keep the voice warm, plain, human)
- "Life happens. That's exactly what we're here for."
- "Couldn't get it back this time? No hard feelings — the door's always open."
- "A pawn loan doesn't follow you home. When it's done, it's done."
- "No credit check. No collections. No shame. Just help when you need it."
- "You trusted us once. We'd be glad to help again — same as always."
- "Need cash again? Want to find something good? Either way, come see us."

### Hard DON'Ts (these break the whole thing)
- **Never** reference the specific lost item or its category ("your gold ring," "that guitar") — it stings and it can feel surveillance-y.
- **Never** imply they did something wrong, or "let it go," or "missed your chance."
- **No** desperate / predatory "FAST CASH NOW" energy — that's the opposite of the brand.
- **No** discount, coupon, or money offer (locked decision — this is relationship-only). A discount would accidentally signal "we're trying to make up for something," which re-frames the loss as a wound.
- **No** "we miss you / where've you been?" guilt framing — it puts the burden back on them.
- **No** "Dixie Pawn" references; Harrisonburg is Valley Pawn.
- For Roanoke: **never** firearms language in any digital channel.

The emotional target on every touch: *relief + welcome*, never *guilt + obligation*.

## Channels & their jobs

The plan runs on two layers:

- **Always-on (brand-level, public):** social, GBP, website, and email tone — quietly normalizes pawn-as-a-tool for *everyone*, so the forfeited customer who's lurking sees "no shame" messaging in the wild without ever being singled out.
- **Direct (1:1, private):** Bravo push, Chekkit SMS, and segmented email — reaches the actual forfeited-loan customer by name, gently, at the right moment.

| Channel | Layer | Role in the win-back | Reach to forfeited customers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bravo push** | Direct | The lightest, most native touch — app notification, feels like a service message, not an ad | App users only |
| **Chekkit SMS** | Direct | The workhorse — texts our own store numbers, where these customers already reach us | Anyone with a phone on file (consent rules apply) |
| **Email (Brevo)** | Direct + brand | Carries the fuller "here's how pawn actually protects you" story; segmentable | Anyone with email on file |
| **Instagram / Facebook** | Always-on | Normalizes the message publicly via Story/Heritage pillars; no targeting needed | Passive — they see it in feed |
| **Google Business Profile** | Always-on | Catches them at high intent ("pawn shop near me") with trust-first posts | Passive — search moment |
| **Website (thevalleypawn.com)** | Always-on | A permanent "How pawn works / what happens if you can't repay" page that removes fear *before* the first loan, and reassures after | Passive — anytime |

### 1. Bravo push notifications (Direct — lightest touch)

**Job:** A single, soft, service-style nudge. Because it arrives in the app the customer already chose to install, it reads as "from my pawn shop," not "marketing."

**Cadence:** Sparingly. One re-engagement push, then only fold them into normal seasonal/value pushes. Over-pushing app users is the fastest way to an uninstall.

**Example push copy (short — push gets truncated):**
- *"Valley Pawn is here whenever you need us — cash, deals, no judgment. Tap to see what's in."*
- *"Life happens. We're still here for you, same as always. 👋"*
- *"Need a hand again? No credit check, no catch. We've got you."*

**Note:** Confirm in `bravo-context` what targeting Bravo push supports (all app users vs. by loan status). If Bravo can't segment to *forfeited* loans specifically, run this as a gentle brand-wide push so no one is singled out — which actually fits the "no shame" ethos better.

---

### 2. Chekkit SMS (Direct — the workhorse)

**Job:** Chekkit already owns our outgoing text + inbound messaging across all 5 store numbers. These customers text our store numbers as much as they call (per the email-CTA rule). A short, warm, *personal-feeling* text from "their" store is the single highest-leverage touch in this plan.

**Compliance first (non-negotiable):**
- Only text numbers with prior business relationship + SMS consent. TCPA applies. Promotional texts have quiet-hours rules (no texts before 8am / after 9pm local) and must honor STOP/opt-out.
- Keep it relationship/service-toned, not a blast. Send from the store the customer actually used, signed by a real first name where possible (we have the employee directory) — it reads human, not robotic.
- Stagger sends by store so replies are answered live in Chekkit.

**Example SMS copy (warm, plain, no item reference):**
- *"Hey [First] — it's [Name] at Valley Pawn in [City]. Just wanted you to know the door's always open here, no strings. If you ever need cash or want to come browse, we'd be glad to see you. — text or call us anytime at this number."*
- *"[First], this is Valley Pawn [City]. No catch, no sales pitch — just a hello and a reminder that we're here whenever you need us. Same fair deal as always. 👋"*
- *"Life gets expensive. When it does, we're an easy, no-judgment option — no credit check, nothing follows you home. We're right here at [City] whenever you're ready."*

**Cadence:** One re-engagement text. If no reply, leave them be for ~90 days, then they roll into normal seasonal Chekkit touches. Never a drip of guilt.

### 3. Email (Brevo — the full story)

**Job:** Email is where we can actually *explain* the reframe — that a pawn loan is the safest form of borrowing they have access to, and that forfeiture is a clean break, not a black mark. This is the "here's why you should feel good, not bad" piece.

**Build on what exists:** Use the **VP Master Template (ID 11)** — duplicate, swap the 10 markers, ship. All locked blocks (logo, warranty strip, 5-store directory with Call/Text per store, hours, DBA-only footer) stay. Do **not** hand-roll HTML.

**Audience:** Build a Brevo segment of forfeited-loan customers (sourced from Bravo, imported/tagged in Brevo). If clean forfeiture-status data isn't exportable, fall back to the broader "Past customers — no real open in 90 days" segment (ID 1) — same message lands fine on any lapsed customer and singles no one out.

**Suggested email — "Here whenever you need us" (one evergreen, sent ~quarterly to the segment):**
- **Eyebrow:** `STILL HERE FOR YOU`
- **Headline:** `No judgment. Just help when you need it.`
- **Subline:** `A pawn loan is the rare kind of borrowing that never follows you home — and our door is open the same as it always was.`
- **Body beats:** (1) what pawn actually is — collateral, no credit check, no credit-score hit; (2) when a loan ends, it ends — no debt, no collections, nothing lingering; (3) you're always welcome, to borrow or just to browse; (4) warm sign-off.
- **Primary CTA:** `Find your store` → store directory / maps. *(No "redeem" or "claim" language — there's nothing to claim.)*

**Cadence:** Evergreen send ~once a quarter to the segment, plus this tone bleeds into the normal Loans-template sends around bill cycles (15th / month-end) so the "no shame" frame is consistently present, not a one-off.

### 6. Website (Always-on — kill the fear at the source)

**Job:** A permanent, reassuring **"How a Pawn Loan Works — and What Happens If You Can't Repay"** page on thevalleypawn.com. This does double duty: it removes the fear *before* someone's first loan (more loans), and it's the soft landing we can link from email/SMS for anyone who wants the full story without a conversation.

**Page should plainly answer the scary questions:**
- "What if I can't pay it back?" → You simply don't get the item back. No debt, no collections, no credit damage. That's it.
- "Will this hurt my credit?" → No. Pawn loans never touch your credit.
- "Will you come after me?" → No. A pawn loan ends cleanly — nothing follows you.
- "Can I come back after I lost an item?" → Always. You're a valued customer, full stop.

SEO note: this page targets real, high-volume worry-queries ("does pawn affect credit," "what happens if you don't pay back a pawn loan") — strong organic + trust value. Link it from the GBP profile, the email CTA, and a website nav/footer link.

## Audience & data

**Primary segment:** customers with a forfeited pawn loan in Bravo, ideally last 12–24 months (recent enough to re-engage, past the immediate sting).

**Data flow to confirm (`bravo-context`):**
1. Can Bravo export a forfeited-loan customer list with contact fields (name, store, phone, email, app-user flag, SMS consent)?
2. Push that list into a **Brevo segment** and a **Chekkit audience**, tagged so we never accidentally reference the item.
3. **Consent gate:** filter to SMS-consented numbers before any Chekkit send; filter to subscribed/non-suppressed for email.

**If forfeiture status isn't cleanly exportable:** default to "lapsed customer" (no activity 6–24 mo) as the audience. The message is universal enough that it lands well on any lapsed customer and avoids singling anyone out — which is on-brand for "no shame."

### How we pull it — through the Bravo Data Extraction pipeline (not by hand)

The right way to get this list is the same **additive-only pipeline** that already runs the loan/layaway, aged-inventory, and EOM reports — never a manual screen-scrape each month. The pattern (per `bravo-context`):

1. **New saved Ad Hoc report in Bravo** — `Loans/Buys → Custom Reports → Bravo Ad Hoc Loan Report Generator`. Name it project-specific, e.g. **"Claude Forfeited Winback"** (do **not** edit an existing saved report). Filter on **forfeited/expired loan status** within a rolling window (e.g. last 24 months), and select customer-contact columns: first name, store, phone, email, app-user flag, last-payment/forfeiture date, and any SMS/email consent field Bravo exposes.
2. **Clone a handler** — copy an existing AHK handler (e.g. `LoanReviews.ahk`) to a new file like `ForfeitedWinback.ahk` with its own function name pointing at the new saved report.
3. **Register a new pipeline cell** — add a `forfeited-winback` row to the `bravo_watcher.ahk` / `bravo_export.ahk` dispatch tables (ADD lines, never edit existing ones), then restart the watcher.
4. **Run across all 5 stores** via `bravo-store-cycle` → drops `<DATE>_<STORE>_forfeited-winback.csv` per store into the pipeline `output/` folder.

This is a one-time build (needs a Bravo/Parallels computer-use session). After that, it's a single trigger — repeatable, consistent, zero hand-scraping.

**Consent caveat to verify during the build:** Bravo may not store *marketing* SMS/email consent on the customer record (only the transactional relationship). If the Ad Hoc report doesn't expose a clean consent flag, we gate consent on the Brevo/Chekkit side instead — Brevo's subscribed/non-suppressed status, and Chekkit's own opt-in list — before any send.

### Monthly automation (the part that makes it run itself)

Wrap the above in a **monthly scheduled task** (e.g. 1st of each month):
1. Trigger the `forfeited-winback` cell across all 5 stores.
2. **Merge + dedupe** the 5 CSVs into one master, key on customer ID/phone.
3. **Diff against last month's master** → splits the audience into *new this month* (just forfeited) vs. the *rolling pool* (forfeited in the trailing 24 mo). New-this-month get the first warm touch; the rolling pool get the lighter ongoing rhythm.
4. **Consent-filter**, then sync to the **Brevo segment** + **Chekkit audience**.
5. Hand off to the monthly send (see cadence below). Post a short summary to Slack so you can see counts each month.

This mirrors how the Monday combined run and daily funds verification already work — CSV-in, automated-action-out, no live Bravo babysitting after the build.

## Long-term cadence (this is a posture, not a campaign)

We engage this audience **monthly** — but the key to monthly *without* nagging is to **rotate the channel and the angle each month** so no single person feels hit on every channel at once. One warm touch per month, varied, is "they're consistently there"; the same message on every channel every month is pressure. The monthly Bravo pull (above) feeds it automatically.

**Monthly rotation (repeats on a quarter cycle):**

| Month in cycle | Direct touch (the one monthly 1:1) | Always-on (public, runs every month regardless) |
|---|---|---|
| **Month 1** | Evergreen **email** — "Here whenever you need us" (VP Master Template) | 1–2 Story/Heritage no-shame social posts; 1 trust-first GBP post/store |
| **Month 2** | Warm **Chekkit SMS** from the customer's store | (same always-on social + GBP rhythm) |
| **Month 3** | Light **Bravo push** to app users + a *different* email angle (e.g. "how pawn protects your credit") | (same always-on social + GBP rhythm) |
| **…repeat** | Cycle restarts at email | — |

**New-this-month forfeitures** (from the pipeline diff) always get the email touch first — the fuller "you should feel fine about this" story — before they enter the rotation. Seasonal stress points (post-holiday January, tax season, back-to-school August) get the warmest version of whatever that month's touch is, since that's when people actually need cash.

**Frequency discipline:** monthly = ~12 direct touches/year, so the **rotation is the safety valve** — any one person gets roughly one email *or* one text *or* one push in a given month, not all three. **Opt-out / unsubscribe rate is the brake**: if SMS opt-outs or email unsubs tick up, drop the cadence to every-other-month for the rolling pool (keep new forfeitures monthly). Watch it the first 90 days closely.

## Compliance guardrails (read before any direct send)

- **SMS (Chekkit):** TCPA — prior express consent required; honor STOP; quiet hours (no sends 9pm–8am local). Send from the store relationship that exists. Don't bulk-blast cold numbers.
- **Email (Brevo):** CAN-SPAM — valid physical address (Culpeper line satisfies it), working unsubscribe, DBA-only footer (never "Full Circle Finance Inc"). Respect the suppression list.
- **No item-level personalization** anywhere — both for tone and to avoid any appearance of using their loss as a marketing hook.
- **Roanoke:** no firearms language in any digital channel.

## How we'll know it's working (KPIs)

This is a trust play, so metrics skew toward *return behavior*, not click-through. Track over rolling quarters:

| Metric | Where | What good looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Re-activation rate** — forfeited customers who transact again (loan, buy, or sale) | Bravo | The north star. Any lift over baseline lapse behavior is a win. |
| Calls + Texts per 1,000 (from email/SMS) | Brevo UTM + Chekkit | ≥ 8, trending up (matches existing email north-star) |
| SMS reply rate / positive sentiment | Chekkit inbox | Replies and warmth > opt-outs |
| Opt-out / unsubscribe rate | Chekkit / Brevo | SMS opt-out low; email unsub < 0.5% — **the health red-line.** If this climbs, we're pushing too hard; back off. |
| Website page traffic + time-on-page | GBP/site analytics | Organic growth on the "how it works" page |
| Review volume + "treated me with respect" themes | GBP / Chekkit | Rising — the public proof of no-judgment |
| "How'd you hear about us?" at the counter | Bravo customer notes | Attribution for real foot traffic |

**Baseline first:** before launch, pull the current re-transaction rate of forfeited customers from Bravo so we have a number to beat.
